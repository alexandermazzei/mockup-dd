Key Findings

This Quality of Earnings report reveals several important findings about ABC Corp's financial performance. Overall, the company demonstrates solid fundamentals but with specific areas requiring attention:

  • Revenue Recognition Issues: Identified $425,000 in revenue that was prematurely recognized in FY 2024 for multi-year contracts. This has been adjusted to properly allocate across the contract periods.
  • Non-Recurring Income: Discovered $320,000 in one-time, non-operational gains that were included in reported EBITDA. These have been removed to reflect sustainable earnings.
  • Unrecorded Liabilities: Uncovered $180,000 in unrecorded expenses/liabilities that should have been recognized in FY 2024.
  • Owner-Related Expenses: Identified $125,000 in owner-related expenses that should be added back as they are discretionary and non-recurring.
  • Bank Reconciliation: All reported income has been reconciled to bank statements with no unexplained variances exceeding materiality thresholds.

Conclusion

After our comprehensive analysis and adjustments, we've determined that ABC Corp's adjusted EBITDA for FY 2024 is $9.8 million, which is 7.5% lower than the reported figure of $10.6 million. While this represents a material adjustment, the company's underlying earnings quality remains strong, with sustainable growth trends evident in the core business operations.

About Quality of Earnings Reports

A Quality of Earnings (QoE) report is a financial forensic audit that reviews and reconciles all income and expenses to bank statements. It provides credibility and guarantees to buyers and lenders that the business's accounting and profit/losses have been reviewed by someone other than the seller. Traditionally, QoE reports are backed by large financial firms, providing additional credibility. As this platform develops, we'll continue to enhance verification processes to build similar trust and reliability.
